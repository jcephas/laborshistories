Andrei Platonov's Kotlovan, 1930

Andrei Platonov's Kotlovan is considered the first surrealist novella. Written during early-stage Stalinist collectivisation, Platonov depicts the futile struggle of a group of workers as they dig what they believe to be the foundation pit for a home for the proleriat, but turns out to be their own graves.

Summary

The story begins when Voshchev (in the 30th year of his personal life) is laid off from the factory for inattention and pensiveness. He leaves his city and, while sleeping rough, he encounters a group of workers who have been tasked with digging a foundation pit, upon which, he is told, an enormous building to house the proletariat will be built.

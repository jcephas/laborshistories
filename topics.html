Space and Subjectivity: Labor's Histories

Topics

Working (Class) Bodies

The human subject is, fundamentally, a body. Bodily dispositions are highly charged with social meaning, leading to an equivalence between the physical spaces and social spaces in which bodies labor. How is labor embodied? How is social class made manifest through bodily dispositions?

Dante Holly Isabelle Kathleen Keren Ladi'Sasha Max Nigel Patrick Rebecca Sophia Vanessa Win Yohana
Racialized Labor

Race is a willfully constructed concept, an idea, that has a distinct and traceable history. Much of that history is inextricably bound to the history of labor under capitalism. What is racial capitalism and how has it racialized labor?

Dante Holly Isabelle Kathleen Keren Ladi'Sasha Max Nigel Patrick Rebecca Sophia Vanessa Win Yohana
Imag(in)ing Labor

The imaging of labor has played a central role in communicating values associated with working bodies and the spaces of labor. How has labor been visualized and represented? Who has been representing labor and what social responsibilities do those representations assume? What does labor “look like” in built environment histories?

Dante Holly Isabelle Kathleen Keren Ladi'Sasha Max Nigel Patrick Rebecca Sophia Vanessa Win Yohana
Architecture's Labor

Questions of labor rarely appear in architectural histories.What are labor’s histories in architecture? What kinds of work might labor histories of architecture reveal? How do architectural design and architectural history address the work of architects? What is the nature of architectural labor?

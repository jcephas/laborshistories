(COUNTER-)TELEOLOGIES & DE/ONTOLOGISING LANGUAGE:

Andrei Platonov's Kotlovan (1930) in the discursive context of early Stalinist collectivisation

The early Stalinist period fractured reality between labour and language. In a departure from Marxist and early Soviet thought, Stalinist discourse attributed an ontologically causal role to language integrating it within the socioeconomic base. As a result, the Marxist teleology of labour was discursively replaced by a Stalinist attempt to ontologise language as reality. In what follows, I will investigate how this transformation of the relationship between language and labour occurs, its manifestation in the discourse surrounding early collectivisation, and then move on to examine Andrei Platonov’s novella Kotlovan (‘The Foundation Pit’, 1930) as a response both to the praxis of collectivisation and to the theories of labour and language that attend it.

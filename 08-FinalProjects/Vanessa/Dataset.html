Dataset

The dataset compiled for this project has gathered data on spaces that have directly been built or transformed by Puerto Ricans throughout New York City. The dataset particularly focuses on the Bronx and Manhattan, as these two boroughs are home to major historically Puerto Rican neighborhoods such El Barrio (East Harlem), Loisaida (Lower East Side), and the South Bronx. Sourced primarily from Google Map searches and reference material cited in the bibliography, the coordinates provide the locations for either existing or once existing locales that served as spaces of resistance, community, and/or living. The data is classified into different spatial types including: casitas (which has been grouped with community gardens for the purposes of mapping, given the site relationship of casitas with gardens), dome, gallery, demonstration, center, and building. These types have emerged as prominent architectural sites formed through bregas colectivas. These sites reflect how the subjects and architects of these spaces were constantly renegotiating dynamics of power and labor, in order to create defined areas where they could regenerate.

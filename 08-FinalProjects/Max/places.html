places

Mariposa Company was a corporation controlled by financiers based in New York; the Company owned the title and mineral rights to the seventy square-mile land grant called Mariposa Estate ("Las Mariposas" prior to 1848). The Company and Estate were legally distinct entities. This transcontinental business arrangement enabled speculators to float the Estate's mineral deposits on financial markets. These are the spaces comprising this network of money, land, and labor.

Final project by Maxwell Smith-Holmes for Architecture's Subjects: Labor's Histories [ARC550]

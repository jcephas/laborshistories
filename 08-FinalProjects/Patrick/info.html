carceral craftsmanship in a forest of products

About the author

Patrick Jaojoco is a PhD candidate in the History and Theory of Architecture at Princeton University. My work considers the role of 20th century social, environmental, and infrastructural experimentation in crafting a postcolonial Philippine society amenable to neoliberal globalization.

Read more here.

About the project

This project was conceived, designed, and created by Patrick Jaojoco for the class ARC 550: Space and Subjectivity: Labor’s Histories with Dr. Jay Cephas at Princeton University, Spring 2023. The maps depicted in the section entitled “carceral networks” were also conceived, designed, and created by Patrick Jaojoco with guidance from Dr. Cephas.

This project elaborates upon past reading and research conducted with guidance from Dr. Wendy Warren in a seminar entitled HIS 579: Readings in Carceral History at Princeton University, Fall 2022.

Screening Foreign Labor

The clip above is from a film created in 1983 by the VEB Braunkohlenkombinat Senftenberg, a major lignite mine in present-day Saxony. Vertragsarbeiter at the Braunkohlenkombinat were employed in a range of positions, from operating mining machinery to training as technicians and mining engineers.

This group, likely among the first to arrive in East Germany, is shown departing a flight at Berlin’s Schoenefeld Airport and boarding buses to Senftenberg. As they climb onto the bus, they cast glances—some hesitant, some confident—at the camera. Once on board, a number of new Vertragsarbeiter break out in song. While a clearly hopeful portrait of life in East Germany, this footage is also notable for demonstrating the propaganda potential of the Vertragsarbeiter program.

Footage: This footage was reproduced on the website of the 2020 "Bruderland" project, overseen by Dr. Isabel Enzenbach at the Technical University of Berlin. See more of the project here.

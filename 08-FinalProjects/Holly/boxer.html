Sport and Leisure

One of the ways in which Vertragsarbeiter could experience East German society was through sport. Above is a newpaper clipping from Neues Deutschland, the offical newspaper of the SED party, describing a victory by the Mozambican boxer Jua Luciano Louis. According to this text, 21-year old Louis had won Berlin's city-wide boxing tournement just weeks after successfully passing his Facharbeiterprüfung ("skilled worker examination"). Louis had entered the GDR as a Vertragsarbeiter in June 1981, and had trained at a slaughterhouse near Berlin, passing a test which included "cutting out half a pig and making salami and knackwurst." In addition to boxing, Louis reportedly studied Spanish and English. The clipping notes that Louis planned to return to Mozambique in June 1985, where he would continue to box and hope to one day represent his home country in the Olympic Games.


Other Vertragsarbeiter enjoyed Fußball as their primary hobby outside of work. Vertragsarbeiter were not permitted to form independent teams, but they were allowed to organize teams within an existing East German soccer club. Vertragsarbeiter teams organized tournements and traveled for game play on their days off from work, often playing teams made up of other foreigners [1].

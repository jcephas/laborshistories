Below is a translation of the text of the treaty signed in Maputo on February 24, 1979. Honecker’s visit to Maputo was notable enough to appear in the Western press. The text of the treaty, which outlines the basic principles of cooperation between the GDR and Mozambique, laid the foundation for the development of the Vertragsarbeiter agreement shortly after.

Treaty of Friendship and Cooperation between the German Democratic Republic and the People's Republic of Mozambique

The German Democratic Republic and the People's Republic of Mozambique, beginning with the fraternal friendship and cooperation between their parties and peoples already forged during the Armed Struggle for National Liberation of the Mozambican People and based on Marxism-Leninism and Proletarian Internationalism;
Guided by the desire to contribute to the strengthening of the natural anti-imperialist alliance between Socialist States and the National Liberation Movements and to the further unification of all with the deepening of fraternal friendship and cooperation between the German Democratic Republic and the People's Republic of Mozambique and to contribute to forces fighting for peace, democracy, and social progress;
Imbued with the ideals of struggle against imperialism, colonialism, neocolonialism, racism, and apartheid;
Determined to contribute to strengthening the peace and security of all peoples;
Willing to further develop the social and economic achievements of both countries and to support each other in doing so;
Reaffirm their loyalty to the purposes and principles of the Charter of the United Nations; Agree to sign this Treaty of Friendship and Cooperation and agree as follows:

Article 1
The High Contracting Parties solemnly declare their determination to strengthen and expand the friendship between their two countries and peoples, and to work together in the interest of advancing the socioeconomic achievements of their peoples. They will develop their political, economic, scientific, technical, and cultural relations guided by the principles of respect for sovereignty, territorial integrity, non-interference in internal affairs, and equality. They will intensify cooperation and direct contacts between the political and social organizations of both countries.

Article 2
The High Contracting Parties will make every effort to expand mutually beneficial economic cooperation and exchanges of experiences in the fields of industry, agriculture, fishing, transport, communications, training of military cadres, and to deepen other areas. Both States will cooperate in the field of trade based on the principles of equality, mutual benefit, and most-favored-nation (Meistbegünstigung) treatment.

Article 3
The High Contracting Parties will cooperate in science and technology, art, literature, education, healthcare, press, radio, film, sport, and other fields to encourage the mutually beneficial knowledge of the lives and achievements of both peoples.

Article 4
The German Democratic Republic and the People's Republic of Mozambique pursue a policy of Peace aimed at strengthening friendship and cooperation between all peoples.
The German Democratic Republic values the peace policy of the People's Republic of Mozambique as a key factor in maintaining world peace, for detente, and for international security and appreciates the policy of non-alignment practice by the People's Republic of Mozambique.
The People's Republic of Mozambique values the peace policy of the German Democratic Republic as an essential factor in maintaining world peace, detente, and international security.

Article 5
In the interest of strengthening the High Contracting Parties’ defense capabilities, they will regulate cooperation in the military field via bilateral agreements.

Article 6
The High Contracting Parties will continue the struggle for World Peace and for the strengthening of international security. They are making efforts to deepen international detente and to extend it to all regions and make it irreversible. In order to banish war from people's lives once and for all they advocate for general and complete disarmament, including nuclear, under effective international control.

Article 7
The High Contracting Parties advocate the creation of a new international economic order on an equal and democratic basis, free from imperialist exploitation. They support the sovereign right of peoples to dispose of their natural resources.

Article 8
The High Contracting Parties will continue to fight consistently against the forces of imperialism for the final elimination of fascism, colonialism, neo-colonialism, racism, and apartheid. They are committed to the full implementation of the United Nations Declaration on the Granting of Independence to Colonial Countries and Peoples. They support the right of peoples to freely choose their path of development. Both States support the people's just struggle for freedom, national independence, and social progress, and will make joint efforts to cooperate with all other peace-loving states to achieve these goals.

Article 9
The High Contracting Parties will exchange information and opinions and hold consultations at various levels for the purpose of deepening and expanding their cooperation, coordinating foreign policy activities, and discussing international issues in mutual interest to both sides.

Article 10
If a situation arises that threatens or violates the peace, the High Contracting Parties shall communicate with each other to eliminate their positions of the accrued danger or to vote for the restoration of peace.

Article 11
Each of the High Contracting Parties solemnly declares that it will not enter into any alliances or participate in any action directed against the other High Contracting Party.

Article 12
The High Contracting Parties declare that the obligations of this treaty are not inconsistent with any earlier international Treaties, and do not undertake any other International to enter into any agreement that is inconsistent with this contract.

Article 13
Any question arising between the High Contracting Parties as to the interpretation or application of any provision of this treaty shall be settled by direct bilateral negotiations in a spirit of friendship, mutual respect, and understanding.

Article 14
This treaty is subject to ratification and takes effect on the day of exchange of the instruments of ratification and power in Berlin, the capital of the German Democratic Republic.

Article 15
This treaty has a term of 20 years and will be automatically renewed for 5-year periods, unless one of the High Contracting Parties expresses in writing 12 months before the expiration of the period of validity a wish to terminate it.

Completed in Maputo, February 24th 1979, in two copies, in both the German and Portuguese languages. Both texts are equally valid.

For the
German Democratic Republic
Erich Honecker
General Secretary of the Central Committee of the Socialist Unity Part of Germany
And chairman of the state council of the German Democratic Republic

For the
People's Republic of Mozambique
Samora Moises Machel
President of the FRELIMO party and President of the People’s Republic of Mozambique

In effect August 23, 1979.

Printed in the Law Gazette of the GDR, 11 (1979) no. 4 pg 60-61.

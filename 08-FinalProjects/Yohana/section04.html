Poetry as Evidence

The following section contains early work that attempts to read against the grain of the aforementioned 19th century literature. It utilizes redaction as a poetic method to allow new narratives to emerge from existing travel accounts, letters, poems, hagiographies, treaties, and so on. These documents were written around the time of the Zaldagar event, though not all of them write about their contemporary time. I’ve clung onto these documents not because I believe they can serve as evidence of a past violence (though, not long ago I thought they might), but rather because the past violence is a present-past; it lives with me today, and both my participation and its inclusion in a larger academic-institutional network require that we (the documents and I) familiarize ourselves with one another. Redaction, then, becomes a poetic method to write into existence the archive that cannot exist otherwise. It reveals in its simultaneous obfuscation.
